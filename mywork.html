Photo Editing at the Columbia Missourian

I was the lead project outreach coordinator and photo editor for the music beat at the Columbia Missourian, where I made connections with a variety of local concert venues, scheduled events, and started up a beat that had not existed for as far back as I can find at the Missourian. I also worked as one of the photo editors in the social justice department, which was an inspiring and humbling experience that brought me closer to the community.

1. Post Sex Nachos at the Blue Note

This is possibly my favorite photo edit ever. Kate Trabalka, an incredible Missourian photographer, went on a mission for these photos. I had to coordinate with my contact at the Blue Note for upgraded access. We chose 12 photos from 3 bands, which required hours of research, toning, captioning, and writing. See the full edit on the site here



2. Art across borders

This came from my work on the social justice beat at the Missourian. A beautiful story about Cristina Núñez, a painter from Venezuela who moved to Missouri. It was part of our immigrant profile series. See the full edit on the site here



3. A Field Day with Local Bees

This was an edit that fell outside my jurisdiction on the music and social justice beats. However, I was lucky to be on staff this day for a fun project! Josh Ellenburg, the photographer, found a local bee meeting he wanted to photograph. While Josh did all of the grunt work on this one, I was fortunate to have experience raising bees in the past and be able to provide my own input. The final gallery of photos left me in the photo lab for quite a late night, but it was all worth it. See the full edit on the site here
